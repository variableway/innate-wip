## The Real Problem

Every company talks about "bridging design and engineering." Most solutions focus on skills: teach designers to code, teach engineers design fundamentals.

But the gap isn't about skills. It's about **context**.

### What Designers Don't See

- The technical debt that makes "simple" changes expensive
- The performance implications of animation choices
- The edge cases that only emerge at scale

### What Engineers Don't See

- The user research behind a seemingly "wrong" design decision
- The emotional arc of an interaction flow
- The brand consistency reasons for specific spacing or color choices

## The Solution: Shared Context

Instead of teaching each other's skills, we should share each other's **context**:

1. **Design reviews with engineering present.** Not to approve, but to understand.
2. **Architecture decisions explained in user-facing terms.** Not "we're using ISR" but "the page loads faster for returning visitors."
3. **Shared documentation.** Design specs and technical specs live in the same place, cross-referenced.

## What We Practice

At Innate, every feature starts with a **context doc** — a short write-up that explains the why from both design and engineering perspectives. It takes 15 minutes to write and saves hours of back-and-forth.

> The best products aren't designed and then built. They're designed *while* being built, with full context flowing both ways.
